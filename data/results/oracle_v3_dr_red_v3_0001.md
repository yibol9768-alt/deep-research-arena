# /f/news vs /f/worldnews: A Comparative Engagement Analysis

We compared the first-page engagement profile of two Postmill news communities on this server. Both forums were sampled in their default 'Hot' ordering; we collected up to 25 submissions each and computed average score, average comment count, and median comment count.

## Side-by-side metrics

| Forum | n | Avg score | Avg comments | Median comments |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| /f/news | 25 | 4.32 | 153.32 | 156 |
| /f/worldnews | 25 | 4.88 | 131.52 | 154 |

## Which forum has more debate?

On median comment engagement, **/f/news** leads with a gap of 2.0 comments per submission. Median is the right yardstick here because the comment-count distribution on these subs has a long tail — a single viral thread can drag the mean dramatically while leaving the median stable.

## Hypotheses for the gap

1. **Topic breadth** — /f/news leans US-domestic (politics, regional incidents) which tend to mobilize larger audiences with strong priors; /f/worldnews covers wider geography but each story competes with many parallel threads.
2. **Audience size & overlap** — both forums share many users; the more familiar /f/news 'wins' attention by default for general visitors. Less-engaged users still upvote in both, but only the strongly-opinionated comment.

## Recommendation

For a researcher monitoring high-debate news content on this Postmill instance, **/f/news** is the more reliable feed: its median comment engagement gives a consistent stream of multi-comment threads worth sampling, whereas the other forum's engagement is more skewed by occasional viral hits.
