## What the form-factors mean for the buyer

**Over-ear** headphones place padded cups around the entire ear. They isolate noise passively, suit long listening sessions, and typically house larger drivers — but trade portability and warm-weather comfort. **On-ear** headphones rest *on* the ear cartilage. They are lighter and more breathable than over-ears, but tend to leak more sound and are less comfortable past an hour. **Earbud / in-ear** designs are the most portable, work well for commuting and exercise, and dominate the wireless-earbud category — but their tiny drivers limit deep-bass response and isolation depends heavily on tip seal.
