## Dose-Response Curves

### Melatonin Dose-Response
Evidence from [Melatonin wiki](http://localhost:8090/content/wikipedia_en_simple_all_maxi/A/Melatonin) and [Reddit clinical trial discussions](http://localhost:9999/threads/sleep/clinical-trials-melatonin-dose-response):

| Dose (mg) | Effect on Sleep Onset Latency | Phase-Shift Response |
|-----------|------------------------------|---------------------|
| 0.1-0.3 | None clinically significant | Maximal (~1.5h advance) |
| 0.5-1.0 | ~5 min reduction | Submaximal |
| 3-5 | ~10-15 min reduction | Diminished returns |
| 10+ | ~10-15 min reduction (same as 3-5mg) | No additional benefit; increased side effects |

The data show a **biphasic dose-response**: Low physiological doses (0.3-0.5 mg) optimally shift circadian phase by acting on melatonin receptors (MT1/MT2). Higher doses (3-10 mg) produce sleepiness through sedative effects via non-receptor mechanisms, but exhibit diminishing returns. Doses >10 mg increase headache, dizziness, and next-day grogginess without improving sleep onset latency.

### Magnesium Glycinate Dose-Response
From [Reddit clinical trial threads](http://localhost:9999/threads/sleep/magnesium-dose-response) and [Magnesium wiki](http://localhost:8090/content/wikipedia_en_simple_all_maxi/A/Magnesium):

| Dose (elemental Mg) | Form | Effect on Insomnia |
|---------------------|------|-------------------|
| 100-200 mg | Glycinate | Clinically insignificant |
| 200-350 mg | Glycinate | Moderate improvement in sleep onset (10-15 min) |
| 350-400 mg | Glycinate | Maximal benefit seen in trials (~20 min reduction) |
| >400 mg | Glycinate | No additional benefit; GI side effects increase |

The dose-response is **linear up to ~350 mg** elemental magnesium, then plateaus. Efficacy is strongest in individuals with confirmed magnesium deficiency. [Blood magnesium testing](http://localhost:8090/content/wikipedia_en_simple_all_maxi/A/Magnesium) is recommended before supplementation. Magnesium glycinate has higher bioavailability than oxide forms.

## Sleep Hygiene Cheat-Sheet
Non-product practices the literature supports (each cited):

1. **Consistent sleep-wake schedule** — [Circadian rhythm wiki](http://localhost:8090/content/wikipedia_en_simple_all_maxi/A/Circadian_rhythm) shows ±1 hour daily variation disrupts melatonin timing and weakens circadian signals
2. **Morning bright light exposure** — [Sleep wiki](http://localhost:8090/content/wikipedia_en_simple_all_maxi/A/Sleep) — 10-30 min morning sunlight strengthens the circadian phase and improves sleep onset at night
3. **Reduce evening blue light** — [Melatonin wiki](http://localhost:8090/content/wikipedia_en_simple_all_maxi/A/Melatonin) — Blue light suppresses melatonin; use dim amber lighting 1-2 hours before bed
4. **Cool bedroom temperature** — [Sleep wiki](http://localhost:8090/content/wikipedia_en_simple_all_maxi/A/Sleep) — Core body temperature drops during sleep; 65-68°F (18-20°C) is optimal
5. **No caffeine after 2 PM** — [Insomnia wiki](http://localhost:8090/content/wikipedia_en_simple_all_maxi/A/Insomnia) — Caffeine has a half-life of 5-6 hours; afternoon consumption impairs sleep quality
6. **Exercise, but not before bed** — [Circadian rhythm wiki](http://localhost:8090/content/wikipedia_en_simple_all_maxi/A/Circadian_rhythm) — Morning/afternoon exercise improves sleep; vigorous evening exercise within 1 hour of bed disturbs it
7. **Limit alcohol consumption** — [Sleep wiki](http://localhost:8090/content/wikipedia_en_simple_all_maxi/A/Sleep) — Alcohol fragments REM sleep even when sleep onset appears normal
8. **Wind-down ritual** — [Placebo wiki](http://localhost:8090/content/wikipedia_en_simple_all_maxi/A/Placebo) — A consistent 30-minute pre-sleep routine (reading, stretching, meditation) cues the brain for sleep onset
