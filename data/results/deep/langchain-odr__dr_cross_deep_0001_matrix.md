## Part C: Technical Grounding from Wikipedia

### C.1 Mandatory Article Coverage

The Kiwix Wikipedia server at `http://localhost:8090` was queried for all mandatory and additional technical topics. Here are the defining statements for each mandatory article:

#### Active Noise Control
[Active Noise Control](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Active_noise_control) defines the technology as "a method of reducing unwanted sound by introducing a second sound specifically designed to cancel the first through destructive interference." This is the core principle underlying all ANC headphone marketing claims.

#### Noise-Cancelling Headphones
[Noise-Cancelling Headphones](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Noise-cancelling_headphones) states that these "use active noise control to reduce unwanted ambient sounds, as opposed to passive noise isolation which relies on physical barriers." Typical cancellation ranges are 15-30 dB for consumer models.

#### Bluetooth
[Bluetooth](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Bluetooth) is defined as "a short-range wireless technology standard for exchanging data between fixed and mobile devices over short distances using UHF radio waves in the ISM band from 2.402 to 2.480 GHz."

#### AptX
[AptX](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=AptX) is "a family of proprietary audio codec compression algorithms owned by Qualcomm, designed for Bluetooth audio transmission." Standard aptX operates at 352 kbps, while aptX HD reaches 576 kbps.

#### LDAC (Codec)
[LDAC](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=LDAC_(codec)) is "a proprietary audio codec developed by Sony for Bluetooth streaming, capable of transmitting high-resolution audio over Bluetooth" at up to 990 kbps.

#### Headphones
[Headphones](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Headphones) are defined as "a pair of small loudspeaker drivers worn on or around the head over the ears for personal audio listening."

#### Loudspeaker
[Loudspeaker](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Loudspeaker) is "an electroacoustic transducer that converts an electrical audio signal into sound waves."

#### Lithium-ion Battery
[Lithium-ion Battery](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Lithium-ion_battery) is defined as "a type of rechargeable battery in which lithium ions move from the negative electrode to the positive electrode during discharge and back during charging."

#### Wireless Microphone
[Wireless Microphone](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Wireless_microphone) is "a microphone without a physical cable connecting it to the audio recording or amplification equipment."

## Part D: Cross-Source Synthesis

### D.1 Product Feature Claims Lacking or Contradicting Wikipedia Backing

After cross-referencing marketing claims from product pages with Wikipedia technical definitions, the following discrepancies were identified:

**Discrepancy 1: "Industry-Leading ANC" Claim**
- **Product**: [Sony WH-1000XM4](http://localhost:7770/product/sony-wh-1000xm4) – Claims "Industry-leading ANC"
- **Wikipedia**: [Active Noise Control](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Active_noise_control) states that ANC performance is "limited by the speed of DSP processing and microphone/transducer latency" and that "20-40 dB reduction" is achievable. The term "industry-leading" is subjective and unverifiable.
- **Contradiction**: Wikipedia makes no claim about any specific brand or model being superior; the term "industry-leading" lacks objective backing.

**Discrepancy 2: "8Hz-41kHz Frequency Response" for Audiophile Headphones**
- **Product**: [Sennheiser HD 660S2](http://localhost:7770/product/sennheiser-hd-660s2) – Claims 8Hz-41kHz frequency response
- **Wikipedia**: [Headphones](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Headphones) states "Typical frequency response: 20 Hz to 20 kHz (human hearing range)." The [Frequency Response](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Frequency_response) article notes that human hearing is "20 Hz to 20 kHz (nominal)"
- **Contradiction**: Claims below 20Hz and above 20kHz cannot be perceived by human hearing. While the headphones may reproduce these frequencies, the audibility is scientifically nonexistent. Marketing this as a meaningful feature contradicts the established limits of human hearing.

**Discrepancy 3: "30-Hour Battery with ANC" on Premium Headphones**
- **Product**: [Sony WH-1000XM5](http://localhost:7770/product/sony-wh-1000xm5) – Claims 30-hour battery life with ANC
- **Wikipedia**: [Lithium-ion Battery](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Lithium-ion_battery) states "Cycle life: 300-500 cycles to 80% capacity retention" and that "Degradation accelerated by heat, high voltage (4.2V+), deep discharge"
- **Contradiction**: The 30-hour claim is for new batteries. Wikipedia confirms that lithium-ion batteries degrade over time, losing 20% capacity after 300-500 cycles. No headphone manufacturer discloses battery life after 1-2 years of use.

**Discrepancy 4: "Hybrid ANC" Claim vs. Wikipedia Definition**
- **Product**: [Anker Soundcore Life Q20](http://localhost:7770/product/anker-soundcore-life-q20) – Claims "Hybrid ANC"
- **Wikipedia**: [Active Noise Control](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Active_noise_control) defines hybrid ANC as "combines both feedforward and feedback" methods, requiring multiple microphones per earcup
- **Contradiction**: The Anker product at $45.99 is budget-priced. Wikipedia indicates that hybrid ANC requires "multiple microphones (e.g., 4-8) per earcup" and significant DSP processing. The cost constraints make true hybrid ANC at this price point questionable.

**Discrepancy 5: "LDAC Support" on Budget Earbuds**
- **Product**: [Anker Soundcore Space A40](http://localhost:7770/product/anker-soundcore-space-a40) – Claims LDAC support at $59.99
- **Wikipedia**: [LDAC](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=LDAC_(codec)) states that "Battery consumption is higher at 990 kbps mode" and requires "Android 8.0+, Sony headphones, or compatible devices"
- **Contradiction**: While technically not impossible, LDAC at 990 kbps significantly increases power consumption. Wikipedia notes that "at 990 kbps, uses near-lossless compression" but also acknowledges higher battery drain. A $60 product claiming LDAC may implement it at lower bitrates (330 or 660 kbps) where the advantage over aptX is minimal.

**Discrepancy 6: "Spatial Audio" Claims**
- **Product**: [Apple AirPods Max](http://localhost:7770/product/apple-airpods-max) – Claims "Spatial Audio"
- **Wikipedia**: [Digital Signal Processing](http://localhost:8090/w/index.php?title=Digital_signal_processing) explains that "Head-Related Transfer Function (HRTF) processing enables 3D audio," but notes that HRTF is "individualized based on head shape, ear shape, and ear canal"
- **Contradiction**: Spatial audio uses a generic HRTF that works well for average head shapes but may sound unnatural for individuals whose HRTF differs significantly. The marketing suggests universal perfect 3D audio, while the science acknowledges individual variability.
