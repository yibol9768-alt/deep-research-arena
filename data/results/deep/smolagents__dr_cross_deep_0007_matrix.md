## FDA / AAFCO Regulatory Gaps

1. **No mandatory pre-market approval for pet food claims**: Unlike human drugs or animal feeds, pet food marketed with health claims (e.g., "reduces allergies", "natural") does not require FDA pre-approval; companies self-certify (FDA Center for Veterinary Medicine [CMV] guidance). ([FDA Pet Food Regulation](http://localhost:8090/article/fda-pet-food-regulation))
2. **AAFCO labeling standards are not legally binding**: AAFCO's official definitions for terms like 'natural', 'holistic', and 'grain-free' are voluntary guidelines; states may enforce differently, creating patchwork oversight. ([AAFCO Standards](http://localhost:8090/article/aafco-pet-food-standards))
3. **No requirement to prove health benefit for 'diet' claims**: "Grain-free" or "raw" diets can be marketed without clinical trials demonstrating superiority or safety, unlike prescription diets. ([Pet Food Labeling Requirements](http://localhost:8090/article/pet-food-labeling-requirements))

These gaps allow claims from CL1-CL5 to proliferate despite weak or contradictory scientific evidence.
