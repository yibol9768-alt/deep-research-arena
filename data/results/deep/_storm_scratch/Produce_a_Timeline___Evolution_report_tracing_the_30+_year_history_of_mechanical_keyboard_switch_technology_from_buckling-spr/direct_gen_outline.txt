# Timeline of Mechanical Keyboard Switch Technology
## 1980s: The Era of Foundational Technologies
### Buckling-Spring Mechanism
#### IBM Model F (1981): Capacitive buckling-spring design
#### IBM Model M (1985): Membrane-based buckling-spring
#### Key characteristics: Tactile feedback, audible click, high actuation force
### Early Competitors
#### Alps SKCL/SKCM series (1983): Linear and clicky tactile switches
#### Cherry MX prototypes and early production (1984-1985)
#### Foam-and-foil switches (e.g., in Apple, Commodore keyboards)
## 1990s: Cherry MX Ascendancy and Market Consolidation
### Cherry MX Standardization
#### Introduction of MX Blue (tactile/clicky) and MX Black (linear)
#### Expansion into MX Brown (tactile, silent) and MX Red (linear, lighter)
#### Adoption by OEMs: Dell, Logitech, and gaming brands
### Decline of Buckling-Spring and Alps
#### IBM’s shift to rubber dome (e.g., Model M2, 1993)
#### Alps Electric ceases keyboard switch production (mid-1990s)
#### Cherry becomes de facto standard for high-end keyboards
## 2000s: Niche Enthusiast Revival and Cherry MX Dominance
### Mechanical Keyboard Renaissance
#### Rise of online communities (Geekhack, Deskthority, 2007)
#### Custom keyboard builders and group buys
#### Cherry MX remains dominant but faces limited competition
### Clone and Derivative Switches
#### Kailh, Gateron, and other Chinese manufacturers produce MX-compatible clones
#### Cost reduction leads to wider availability
#### Early optical switch experiments (e.g., A4Tech)
## 2010s: Explosion of Variety and Hot-Swap Revolution
### Diversification of Switch Types
#### Kailh Box switches (dust/water resistance, 2017)
#### NovelKeys Cream (linear, POM housing, 2018)
#### ZealPC, Holy Panda, and other boutique tactile designs
### Hot-Swap Sockets
#### Introduction by Glorious, Drop, and others (late 2010s)
#### Enables switch swapping without soldering
#### Standardization of 5-pin PCB mount
### Optical Switches
#### Razer, Logitech, and SteelSeries adopt optical for gaming
#### Faster actuation, longer lifespan (100M+ keystrokes)
#### Light-based actuation eliminates contact bounce
## 2020s: Cutting-Edge Technologies and Ubiquity
### Magnetic Hall Effect Switches
#### Wooting’s Lekker switch (2020): Analog input via magnets
#### Rapid trigger, adjustable actuation point
#### Adoption by Corsair, Razer, and others
### Electrostatic Capacitive (Topre and Hybrids)
#### Topre’s continued niche presence
#### Hybrid capacitive switches (e.g., Niz, Ploopy)
### Further Innovations
#### Low-profile mechanical switches (Cherry MX Low Profile, Kailh Choc)
#### Wireless and programmable keyboards
#### Sustainability efforts: Recycled plastics, modular designs
### Market Expansion and Mainstream Acceptance
#### Mechanical keyboards in office, gaming, and enthusiast segments
#### Switch testing kits and online databases
#### End of Cherry MX monopoly; hundreds of switch types available
## Legacy and Impact
### Influence on Computing and Typing Culture
#### Ergonomics, customization, and user experience
#### Preservation of vintage switch technologies (e.g., Beamspring restoration)
#### Role in open-source firmware (QMK, ZMK)
### Future Directions
#### Further analog and adaptive actuation
#### Integration with haptics and sound customization
#### Environmental and economic challenges
