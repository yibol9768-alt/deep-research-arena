## Claim 1: “PFOA-free non-stick is safe at all temperatures”
### Origin of the claim
### Scientific basis for concern
#### PTFE decomposition temperature and toxic byproducts
#### Thermal degradation onset at 260°C (500°F) and accelerated decomposition above 350°C (662°F)
#### Specific compounds released: carbonyl fluoride, hydrogen fluoride, ultrafine particles
#### Polymer fume fever and inhalation risks
#### Identity of PTFE polymer regardless of PFOA processing aid
### Regulatory and safety agency positions
#### US EPA and FDA statements on PTFE safety limits
#### European Chemicals Agency evaluations
#### Manufacturer maximum operating temperature recommendations vs. independent study findings
### Verdict: Misleading
### Key takeaway for consumers
## Claim 2: “Ceramic-coated pans last as long as PTFE pans”
### Origin of the claim
### Comparison of coating technologies
#### Sol-gel ceramic coating composition (silicon dioxide, titanium dioxide)
#### PTFE (polytetrafluoroethylene) coating structure and thickness
### Published durability testing
#### Abrasion resistance tests
#### Thermal cycling and scratch tests
#### Primary wear mechanism of sol-gel coatings: abrasive degradation vs. PTFE failure modes
#### Consumer reports and real-world lifespan data
### Verdict: False
### Key takeaway for consumers
## Claim 3: “Cast iron skillets contribute meaningful dietary iron”
### Origin of the claim
### Scientific evidence on iron transfer
#### Studies measuring iron leached into food
#### Variables: cooking time, acidity, seasoning layer thickness and integrity, food type
#### Typical iron leaching range (well-seasoned vs. new pan)
#### Comparison to dietary reference intakes (8 mg/day men, 18 mg/day women)
### Health considerations
#### Iron absorption and bioavailability (ferrous vs. ferric forms)
#### Risks for individuals with hemochromatosis and iron overload disorders
### Verdict: Partially true but often overstated
### Key takeaway for consumers

### Reactivity of stainless steel alloys
#### Nickel and chromium leaching mechanisms

### Regulatory standards
#### FDA limits for nickel and chromium
#### EU migration limits
#### Conditions required to approach or exceed safe intake thresholds
### Verdict: Misleading
