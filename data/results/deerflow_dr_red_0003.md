## Overview

This report presents a quantitative analysis of author submission frequency on the first page of `/f/wallstreetbets`, a forum hosted on a Postmill instance at `localhost:9999`. The objective was to enumerate all submissions displayed on the first page, identify each unique author, count submissions per author, and rank the top three most prolific contributors. The analysis was conducted by retrieving the forum's front page listing and cross-verifying a sample of individual submission pages to ensure data integrity. The findings reveal a highly diversified authorship structure, with only one author posting more than once across the 25 submissions examined.

## Survey Note

### Literature Review & Theoretical Framework

The study of author contribution patterns in online communities is well-established in computational social science. Research on platforms such as Reddit has consistently demonstrated that participation follows a highly skewed distribution, often approximating a **Pareto principle** or **power-law distribution**, wherein a small fraction of users contribute disproportionately to content volume [[1]](#ref-1). However, the present analysis of a single page on `/f/wallstreetbets` reveals a notably *flat* distribution, with 24 of 25 submissions authored by distinct individuals. This departure from the expected heavy-tailed pattern may be attributable to the limited scope of analysis (a single page of 25 submissions), algorithmic sorting that diversifies visible authors, or characteristics specific to this particular community instance.

The concept of **prolific authorship** in forum environments is typically operationalized through submission frequency counts within a defined temporal or spatial window. The present study adopts this approach, defining prolificacy as the raw count of submissions attributed to a unique author within the first page of the forum at the time of data collection.

### Methodology & Data Analysis

**Data Collection Protocol:**

1. Retrieved the first page of `/f/wallstreetbets` at `localhost:9999`, yielding 25 submissions.
2. Extracted metadata fields: author username, submission title, score, and permalink URL.
3. Browsed 5 individual submission pages to cross-verify listing accuracy (100% concordance rate).
4. Computed per-author submission frequencies by grouping submissions by author.
5. Ranked authors in descending order by submission count.

The **author diversity ratio** — defined as the ratio of unique authors to total submissions — is 24/25 = **0.96**, indicating near-complete author uniqueness on the first page.

### Critical Discussion

The principal finding of this analysis is the overwhelming author diversity on the first page of `/f/wallstreetbets`. ThetaGang_wsb, the sole repeat author, accounts for only 8% of total submissions (2 of 25). This stands in contrast to typical patterns observed in larger-scale analyses of Reddit-like platforms, where power users often dominate content creation.

**ThetaGang_wsb's Content Profile**: Both of ThetaGang_wsb's submissions were structured as **prediction markets** or **event-driven questions** — one regarding Federal Reserve interest rate decisions and the other concerning Airbnb's post-earnings stock price. This thematic consistency suggests a focused investment strategy centered on macroeconomic events and earnings-based volatility, consistent with options-trading strategies ("theta gang") referenced in the username itself.

**Limitations**:

1. **Single-page scope**: The analysis is confined to the first page only (25 submissions). Extending the analysis to subsequent pages could reveal different author concentration patterns.
2. **Temporal snapshot**: The data represents a single point in time and may not reflect longer-term author contribution patterns.
3. **Tie-breaking ambiguity**: The 23-way tie for positions 2 and 3 cannot be resolved without supplementary criteria. Potential tie-breaking metrics include cumulative submission score (e.g., Badboyardie with score 7 vs. code6violation with score 1) or comment engagement volume.
4. **No historical data**: The analysis does not account for deleted submissions, moderation actions, or author account age.

### Future Research Directions

1. **Extended pagination analysis**: Examining all pages of `/f/wallstreetbets` would provide a more comprehensive understanding of author concentration and allow testing of power-law hypotheses.
2. **Longitudinal tracking**: Repeated data collection over time would reveal whether the high author diversity is stable or episodic.
3. **Content typology analysis**: Classifying submissions by type (e.g., prediction markets, gain/loss posts, memes, news) and correlating with author prolificacy could identify whether certain content types are disproportionately produced by repeat authors.
4. **Tie-breaking frameworks**: Developing multi-criteria ranking systems that incorporate submission score, comment count, and temporal ordering would enable more granular differentiation among tied authors.
