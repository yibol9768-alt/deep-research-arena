**Three concrete hacks drawn from Reddit**:

1. Cast iron > non-stick for beginners — lasts forever, improves with use.

2. Bulk-buy pantry staples (rice, beans, oats) at warehouse stores.

3. One good chef's knife replaces an entire knife block.

## 4. Reinforce vs Challenge Analysis

**Reinforced by Reddit**: Ratings for heavy-duty cookware (cast iron, stainless) align with community praise for durability.
**Challenged by Reddit**: Many trendy kitchen gadgets (avocado slicers, banana peelers, herb strippers) get high storefront ratings but Reddit calls them unnecessary gimmicks. Budget cooks should skip these.

## 5. Minimum Viable Kitchen (5 items)

1. One 10-12 inch skillet (cast iron or stainless) — 80% of home cooking.
2. A chef's knife (8-inch) — the single most-used tool.
3. Large cutting board — protects counter + knife.
4. 3-quart saucepan with lid — soups, boiling, rice.
5. Basic measuring cups + spoons — consistency in recipes.

## 6. Conclusion

The 'minimum viable kitchen' costs under $100 if sourced thoughtfully. The storefront provides many options; Reddit provides the filter that separates genuine essentials from gadgets that collect dust. First-time cooks should resist the urge to buy a pre-assembled 'starter set' and instead curate the 5 essentials above.
