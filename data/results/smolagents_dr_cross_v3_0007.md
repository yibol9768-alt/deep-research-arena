## Part C: 3 Practical Cost-Saving Hacks
*Drawn directly from Reddit community data.*

1.  **The "Prep Immediately" Rule**
    *   **Hack:** Wash and chop your vegetables immediately after returning from the grocery store.
    *   **Why:** A highly upvoted tip on /f/LifeProTips notes that you are significantly more likely to cook if the "work" (chopping) is already done, preventing food from rotting in the crisper drawer [2].

2.  **The "Pantry Challenge"**
    *   **Hack:** Commit to a week where you spend $0 on groceries and only cook using what is currently in your freezer, fridge, and pantry.
    *   **Why:** Users on /f/personalfinance use this to reset their budgets and clear out clutter, forcing creativity before spending money on new ingredients [4].

3.  **Meat as a Garnish, Not a Main**
    *   **Hack:** Instead of a steak or chicken breast as the main event, use meat as a flavoring or side component in a bean or rice dish.
    *   **Why:** Advice from /f/personalfinance suggests that while you don't have to go vegetarian, treating meat as a condiment (like bacon bits or diced chicken in soup) drastically cuts costs without eliminating flavor [3].

If funds are extremely tight, prioritize these 5 items from the research above. These provide the ability to cook a wide variety of meals (breakfast, dinner, grains) with the lowest barrier to entry.
